America’s first global war was the Spanish-American War of 1898. Then came World War I (1917-18), World War II (1941-45), the Korean War (1950-53), the Vietnam War (1955-75, with major U.S. involvement starting in 1964), and the First Gulf War (1991)—all recognized on stamps.

Compared to stamps about the American Revolution or the Civil War, there are far fewer stamps for these wars, with the exception of four ten-stamp souvenir sheets about World War II.
