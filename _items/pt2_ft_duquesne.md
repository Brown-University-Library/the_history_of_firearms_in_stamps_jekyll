The French & Indian War had begun badly for Americans, but it ended well. Here,  the Anglo-Americans capture the French Fort Duquesne. It would soon be  renamed Pittsburgh, in honor of the English parliamentary leader William Pitt.

While the Brown Bess remained the predominant firearm, the rustic American on  the right, wearing animal skins, carries an American Long Rifle. It is also known as  a Pennsylvania-Kentucky rifle. The rifle was invented in the 1730s by German  immigrant gunsmiths, who had come to Pennsylvania for religious freedom.  They modified their heavy central European Jaeger rifles to adapt to American  needs. The rifle became especially popular in the large area of the Ohio River Valley.

Whereas the bore (the interior of the barrel) of musket is smooth, a rifle bore has  spiral grooves that make a bullet spin on its axis. The rifling greatly improves the  bullet’s aerodynamic stability, making it a fine gun for hunting, even at distances  of 200 yards or more.

Compared to a Brown Bess musket, the Pennsylvania-Kentucky rifle was more  difficult to manufacture, slower to reload, longer (and hence more accurate at  distance), lower caliber, and lighter weight. It was superb for the conditions of  the American forest.

Whereas rifles had never been popular in England, the [American Long Rifle](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-prospering-new-republic-1780-to-1860/case-8-romance-of-the-long-rifle/j-graeff-flintlock-rifle.aspx) became the first iconic American firearm.
