This Pony Express rider wears a large handgun, perhaps a [six-shot 1851 Colt percussion revolver](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-nation-asunder-1861-to-1865/case-16-union-pistols-and-revolvers/colt-model-1851-navy-percussion-revolver.aspx).

![A dark revolver with minimal ornamentation, but with a grip of dark wood, and a shining brass trigger-guard](../img/itemimgs/1851colt.webp)

With short barrels and easy handling, revolvers were  well-suited for men or women on horseback. Their  ammunition capacity allowed 5 or 6 shots before reloading. In the Civil War, many cavalrymen on both  sides would be equipped with revolvers. 
This particular handgun belonged to William F. “Buffalo Bill” Cody, who early in his career was a Pony  Express rider.

The 1940 stamp commemorates the 80th anniversary  of the Pony Express. Issued while World War II was  raging in the Eastern Hemisphere, the stamp was perhaps intended in part to remind Americans of their heritage of armed valor and rugged individualism.
