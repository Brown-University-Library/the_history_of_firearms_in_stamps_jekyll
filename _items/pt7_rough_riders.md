This stamp commemorates the 50th anniversary of the Spanish-
American War. Among the participants in America’s all-volunteer
forces were the First Volunteer Cavalry--the Rough Riders--
assembled by Theodore Roosevelt. While they rode to battle, they
fought the Battle of San Juan Hill in Cuba on foot--except for
Roosevelt, who is depicted on the stamp, astride his horse Little
Texas.

San Juan Hill was the decisive battle of the war in Cuba. Roosevelt became a national hero, and was elected Vice-President in 1900. Although the U.S. took some other Spanish
colonies for itself, newly-decolonized Cuba was recognized as an
independent nation.

![U.S. Cavalry issue Colt Single Action Army revolver carried at San Juan Hill. The historic handgun features a 5.5-inch artillery length barrel with an underlying ejector rod, a worn military finish on the frame and six-shot cylinder, a standard single-action hammer, and dark, weathered one-piece walnut grips.](../img/itemimgs/rough_rider_san_juan_hill_colt_saa_revolver.webp)

The photo is [Rough Rider San Juan Hill Colt single action
revolver](https://www.nramuseum.org/the-museum/the-galleries/theodore-roosevelt,-elegant-arms/case-56-america's-splendid-little-war-with-spain-roosevelt's-rough-riders/rough-rider-san-juan-hill-colt-saa-revolver.aspx). In a single action revolver, the user must cock the
hammer with his thumb before pressing the trigger. Roosevelt
himself carried an 1896 Colt double action revolver, which could
be repeatedly fired simply by pressing the trigger. His gun had
previously been on the battleship Maine; while docked in Havana
harbor, the Maine had exploded due to an engine malfunction. At
the time, the explosion was incorrectly blamed on the Spanish,
and was the final straw in causing the Spanish-American War.
