Vermont’s Green Mountain Boys were a hardy militia led by Ethan and Ira Allen. By 1777, the British had decided that New England was impossible to subdue, so they aimed to cut it off from the rest of the country, by capturing the Hudson River Valley. An excellent British force led by General John Burgoyne marched south from Montreal. But their supplies and communications were constantly harassed by the Green Mountain Boys.

The British army was substantially augmented by German mercenaries, known as Hessians. On August 17, 1777, a Hessian foraging party that was attempting to gather supplies from the countryside met the Green Mountain Boys at the Battle of Bennington. The Vermonters’ victory helped set up the turning point of the war, depicted on the next stamps.

As New Englanders, the Green Mountain Boys primarily carried semi-military muskets—multipurpose guns appropriate for combat and for hunting.
