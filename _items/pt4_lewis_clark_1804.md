The stamp was issued on the bicentennial of the great  
expedition.

![This .462 caliber pneumatic firearm features a dark wooden forearm, a round barrel with a tubular magazine along the right side, and a large, bulbous iron buttstock that acts as a pressurized air reservoir. The receiver resembles a flintlock plate but houses an internal pneumatic mechanism.](../img/itemimgs/girardoni_air_rifle_as_used_by_lewis_and_clark.jpg)

Here is another view of the famous [Girardoni](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-prospering-new-republic-1780-to-1860/case-8-romance-of-the-long-rifle/girardoni-air-rifle-as-used-by-lewis-and-clark.aspx).

The Girardoni was expensive, and worth it. Traveling  through a country where Corps of Discovery was far  outnumbered by Indians, the expedition showed off the  rifle’s repeating capabilities to astonished Indians. The  friendly demonstrations made the implicit point that the  smaller group could defend itself against the larger group.
