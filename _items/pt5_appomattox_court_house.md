The famous scene of General Robert E. Lee’s  surrender to General Ulysses Grant, at the Appomattox Court House.

![U.S. Starr Arms Company Model 1858 double-action percussion revolver. The firearm features a dark, blued steel frame, a six-inch round barrel, a six-shot cylinder, a unique curved loading lever beneath the barrel, and a smooth, curved one-piece walnut grip.](../img/itemimgs/u_s_starr_arms_co_1858_double_action.webp)

Officers typically carried handguns, as did artillerymen. So did many cavalrymen. Revolvers from  37 different companies were used in the war. The  picture is of [U.S. Starr Arms Co 1858 Double Action](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-nation-asunder-1861-to-1865/case-16-union-pistols-and-revolvers/us-starr-arms-co-1858-double-action.aspx).  On the Union side, Starr revolvers were eclipsed in  popularity by only revolvers from Colt and Remington.  This revolver’s cylinder is designed to be speedily  removable, so that the user can swap out an empty  cylinder for a preloaded cylinder with a fresh cylinder.  “Double action” means that the revolver can be fired  as fast as the user can press the trigger—as opposed  to “single action,” which requires the user to cock the  hammer with his thumb before each press of the  trigger.
