In this commemorative pane, the stamps with
firearms pictured are: Allied forces retake New
Guinea; D-Day invasion of Normandy, France;
Allies liberate Rome & Paris; Allies clear Saipan
bunkers; and Battle of the Bulge.

![a classic tommy gun, with a wooden stock, rounded drum magazine, and small silencer.](1928tommy.webp)

Everywhere, some American forces were armed
with the [US/Auto Ordnance Model 1928
Thompson Submachine Gun](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/wwii,-korea,-vietnam-and-beyond-1940-to-present/case-40-wwii-more-arms/usauto-ordnance-model-1928-thompson-submachine-gun.aspx). It did not have
long range, but it was very effective within 50
yards. Often, a unit of soldiers armed with
slower rifles would be supported by several men
with Thompsons.
