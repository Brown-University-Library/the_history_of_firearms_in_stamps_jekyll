Korean winters are very harsh.
Even today, American soldiers
stationed in South Korea qualify
for extra pay, due to the hardship
of the climate.

![World War II-era U.S. Winchester M1 semi-automatic carbine. The lightweight military firearm features a short, slender barrel with a single barrel band, a light-colored walnut stock with an integrated oiler slot in the buttstock acting as a sling mount, a parkerized receiver with a small protruding bolt handle, and a short, curved 15-round detachable box magazine.](../img/itemimgs/u_s_winchester_m1_semi_automatic_carbine.jpg)

These soldiers carry the [U.S.
Winchester M1 semiautomatic
carbine](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/wwii,-korea,-vietnam-and-beyond-1940-to-present/case-39-wwii-the-united-states/us-winchester-m1-semi-automatic-carbine.aspx), which had debuted in
World War II, and was still used
in Korea.
