After victory in Brooklyn, the British advanced to Manhattan. Again, the Americans only narrowly escaped complete destruction after being surrounded at Harlem Heights. Meanwhile, the British also advanced on White Plains, a supply depot to the north, in Westchester County. Again, the Americans managed to hold off the British long enough to stage an orderly retreat. Artillery commander Alexander Hamilton helped save the Americans from a rout.

On the lower left of the stamp is the flag with the 13 circling stars, which later became the official flag of the United States. On the right side is another popular flag from the period, with the motto “Liberty or Death,” inspired by Patrick Henry’s famous April 1775 speech to the Virginia legislature. This is the first appearance of that flag on a stamp. The flag is folded so that the word “Death” is not visible.
