The Battle of Oriskany took place on August 6, 1777, in Tryon County, New York. The Tryon county militia was headed to Fort Stanwix (today, Rome, N.Y.), to try to break the British siege there. But on the way, they were ambushed by the British, Loyalist militia, and Indians. General Nicholas Herkimer continued to direct American forces even after he was wounded. Both sides suffered heavy losses, and none of the involved forces were capable of doing much for the rest of the war.
