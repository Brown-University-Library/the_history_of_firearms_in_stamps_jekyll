The explorations of Daniel Boone made it possible for Virginians and North Carolinians to begin settling western regions of their colonies--in what today are the eastern parts of the states of Kentucky and Tennessee.

Although rifles had not caught on in New England, American Long Rifles were the first choice of any frontiersman who could afford one.

By 1774, manufacturing knowledge for the American Long Rifle was widely diffused, as Pennsylvania-trained apprentices had moved south and west. A gunsmith could manufacture an entire rifle from a maple tree and two bars of pig iron.
