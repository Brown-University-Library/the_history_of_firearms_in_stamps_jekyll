A Chiricahua Apache, Geronimo was born in 1829, and by age 17 had become a
talented military leader. He led Apache on raids against Mexicans, Indians, and the
U.S. Army.
When the U.S. government ordered the Chiricahuas to move to a reservation in
1876, Geronimo led the resistance. For the next decade, they carried out a guerilla
war in the harsh terrain of the Southwest.
Finally, only 38 fighters remained. Surrounded by 5,000 U.S. troops at Skeleton
Canyon, Arizona, they surrendered on Sept. 4, 1886. Geronimo was the last Indian
warrior who officially surrendered to the United States, marking the end of the
American Indian wars that had begun in the early seventeenth century.
The renowned warrior became a national celebrity, and rode in President Theodore
Roosevelt’s 1904 inaugural parade. Shortly before his death in 1909, he expressed
regret at not fighting to the death.
