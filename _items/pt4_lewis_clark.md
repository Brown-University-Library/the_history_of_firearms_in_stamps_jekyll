After the Louisiana Purchase doubled the territory of the new United States of America, President Jefferson and Congress sent  the Lewis and Clark Expedition, formally, the “Corps of  Discovery,” to explore it. 

Led by Capt. Merriwether Lewis and 2d Lieutenant William Clark, the expedition was guided most of the way by Sacagawea, the  Shoshone Indian wife of a French fur trader.  

The Corps of Discovery brought plenty of regular firearms, blade arms, and gunpowder. They also carried the most advanced gun  of their time: a 21-shot Girardoni air rifle. Invented in Austria in  1779, this sharpshooter rifle could fire 21 times before needing  to be reloaded. It was powerful enough to take an elk.
