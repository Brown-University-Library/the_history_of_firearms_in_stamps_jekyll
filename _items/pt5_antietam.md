From 2011 to 2015, the Postal Service  commemorated the sesquicentennial of the Civil War.

Part of the Confederate invasion of Maryland (a slave  state that had barely remained in the Union), the  Battle of Antietam was the bloodiest day in all of American military history. 

Among the commanders of the Union forces that day  was General Ambrose Burnside. Despite the racial  prejudices of the time, he was a leader in allowing Black volunteers to fight for freedom. Later, he would  become Governor of Rhode Island, U.S. Senator from  Rhode Island, first President of the National Rifle  Association, and the namesake of “sideburns.” He was also a firearms inventor.
