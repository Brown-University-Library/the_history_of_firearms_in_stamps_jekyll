In 1777, the British began building the frontier Fort Vincennes on the Wabash River, which today is the southern border between Indiana and Illinois. Improvements continued through 1779, and the structure was renamed Fort Sackville. A force of 127 Americans and French led by Col. George Rogers Clark made an arduous 180 mile march in ten days from Kaskasia (in the future state of Illinois) through very wet ground. By the time Clark’s forces arrived, they were nearly out of food. The Americans and French besieged the fort on Feb. 23, 1779. Although the Franco-American forces were initially repulsed, two days later the British surrendered. Colonel Clark was the older brother of William Clark, who later explored the Louisiana Territory. The capture of the fort prevented the British from using it as a base for raids on the Kentucky frontier.
