The Korean War is sometime called “the
forgotten war,” as it came in-between the total
war effort of World War II and the
controversial War in Vietnam of 1965-73.

A Washington, D.C., memorial to the Korean
veterans was erected in 1995. The picture
shows the memorial covered in a D.C.
snowfall, although winter in the District of
Columbia is very mild compared to winter in
Korea.
