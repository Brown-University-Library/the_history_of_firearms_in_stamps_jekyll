This reproduction of Charles Marion
Russell’s painting “Jerked Down”
comes from the 1964 American
Painting Series. In 1880, Russell
moved West and became a cowboy.
From 1888-92, he lived with the
Blackfeet Indians, in the Blood band of
the tribe.

![Smith & Wesson Model No. 3 Second Model single-action revolver. The firearm features a dark blued finish, a long eight-inch ribbed barrel with a top-break latch, a six-shot cylinder with prominent extraction grooves, a case-hardened hammer and trigger guard, and smooth, dark square-butt walnut grips.](../img/itemimgs/smith_wesson_model_no_3_second_model.webp)

In the stamp, the cowboys roping cattle
wear holstered handguns, presumably
typical revolvers, such as the [Smith &
Wesson Model No. 3, Second Model](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/the-american-west-1850-to-1900/case-19-smith-wesson,-marlin,-remington-and-others/smith-wesson-model-no-3-second-model.aspx),
which was manufactured 1870-74.
