One the last battles of the Civil War, Five Forks was  an attempt by General Robert E. Lee’s Army of  Northern Virginia to break out of a Union siege. The  breakout was repulsed by Union cavalry led by Gen. Philip Sheridan (who later became President of the NRA).

![U.S. Spencer Model 1863 lever-action repeating carbine. The firearm features a dark wooden buttstock and matching fore-end, a short blued steel barrel, a dark metal receiver, an external hammer, and a distinctive curved trigger guard lever.](../img/itemimgs/u_s_spencer_lever_action_repeating_carbine.webp)

Cavalrymen preferred carbines (short rifles), which  were easier to handle on horseback, and they  preferred lever actions, which were the easiest rifles to  reload while mounted. Among the leading cavalry  rifles was the [U.S. Spencer Lever Action Repeating Carbine](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-nation-asunder-1861-to-1865/case-14-union-carbines/us-spencer-lever-action-repeating-carbine.aspx). Reloads were very fast, thanks to a special  patented tube that poured seven fresh rounds of  ammunition into the tubular magazine in the rifle’s  buttstock.
