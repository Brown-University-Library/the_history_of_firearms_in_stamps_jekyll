This stamp comes the Post Office’s ill-received 1869 series of pictorials.

Initiated on April 3, 1860, the Pony Express was based in St. Joseph, Missouri, and carried mail station-to-station as far west as Sacramento, California. The Pony Express chopped 10 days off the time it took a letter from the East to reach California via ship sailing around Cape Horn. The Pony Express helped integrate the young State of California into the Union.

However, the Pony Express could never cover its costs, and it was discontinued upon the completion of the transcontinental telegraph line in October 1861.
