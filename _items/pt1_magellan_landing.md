Long before European colonization of  America began, Spanish explorer  Ferdinand Magellan landed in the  Philippines in 1521. Although he was  killed in tribal war, the islands became  a Spanish colony until 1898, when Spain lost the Spanish-American  War. The U.S. granted the Philippines  independence in 1946. This stamp was  issued in the Philippines in 1935, when  the Philippines were a U.S. territory.
