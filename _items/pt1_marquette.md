Note the color difference between this 1968 stamp and the preceding stamp, from 1966. From the issuance of the first  American stamp in 1851 (George Washington, 1 cent) through  the 1960s, American stamps were printed in a single color.  Multiple colors came to American stamps in the 1960s, at about  the same time color televisions were supplanting black & white.
 
In 1668, the Marquette traveled up Canada’s Saint Lawrence River to explore the western area of the Great  Lakes. 

![Full-length profile of a circa 1690 French colonial flintlock fusil with a long wooden stock, alongside a detailed close-up of its metal flintlock firing mechanism.](../img/itemimgs/french_flintlock_fusil.webp)

The pictured firearm is [French trade fusil](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/road-to-american-liberty-1700-to-1780/case-4-shot-heard-round-the-world/french-flintlock-fusil.aspx), a flintlock long gun with relatively lighter weight and a shorter barrel. The French  manufactured many firearms that were intended for trade with  the Indians. As the picture shows, the flintlock’s operating  mechanism was less complicated than the wheellock. By the  mid-17th century, it had become the predominant firearm type  in America.
