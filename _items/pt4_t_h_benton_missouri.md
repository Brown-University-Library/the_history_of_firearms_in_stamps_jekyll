Painter Thomas Hart Benton (1889-1975) was a distant descendant of  Missouri’s first U.S. Senator, and shared the same name. This was the  first stamp to employ modern techniques of full color printing.

The stamp depicts the fraught relationship between Indians and the  settlers crossing the Mississippi River to move West. The settlers are  traveling by ox-drawn covered wagon. They appear startled and worried  by the apparently unexpected arrival of an Indian. He bears a peace  pipe, but are his intentions truly peaceful? The settlers are unsure.

Flintlock firearms were ignited by flint striking steel, and raining a  shower of sparks on gunpowder in a firing pan. The fire in the pan then  traveled to the main gunpowder charge via a small touch hole. Then in  1805, the Scottish Rev. Alexander Forsyth invented percussion ignition.  The gun’s hammer struck a small cap of fulminate that sat on a nipple.  The explosion then ignited the main gunpowder charge inside the  firearms. The percussion cap made firearms more powerful, more  reliable, and faster to reload. The photo shows a [Pennsylvania/Kentucky rifle](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-prospering-new-republic-1780-to-1860/case-8-romance-of-the-long-rifle/pennsylvania-kentucky-flintlock-long-rifle.aspx) that has been retrofitted for percussion ignition.
