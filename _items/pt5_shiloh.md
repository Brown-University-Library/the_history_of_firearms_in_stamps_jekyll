In 1961-65, the Post Office issued a stamp each year for  the centenary of a major Civil War battle.

While this soldier appears to be firing at a distant target, his  rifle holds a large bayonet. In case the battle turns into  hand to hand combat, his rifle can be used as a spear.

![U.S. Springfield Model 1861 Percussion Rifle-Musket, showcasing a full-length dark walnut stock, iron barrel with three bands, percussion lock mechanism, and under-barrel ramrod.](../img/itemimgs/u_s_springfield_model_1861_percussion_rifle_musket.webp)

The photo is a percussion [Model 1861 Springfield Rifle Musket](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-nation-asunder-1861-to-1865/case-15-union-muskets-and-rifles/us-springfield-model-1861-percussion-rifle-musket.aspx), manufactured in 1862. A percussion cap is placed  on the nipple. When the trigger is pulled, the hammer falls  and detonates the percussion cap. Via a small connecting  hole, the cap’s explosion ignites the gunpowder load inside the rifle.
