The third of the 1925 stamps commemorating the beginning of the American  Revolution. The borders of this stamp match the other two, including the crossed  muskets.

Although this stamp is labeled “Lexington-Concord,” the title above the label is  “Washington at Cambridge.” The Continental Congress appointed George  Washington to command the American forces, and he arrived at Cambridge (across  the river from Boston), on July 3, 1775.

By this point, the fighters from New England had been joined by volunteer companies  of rif leman from Pennsylvania and Virginia, who brought their American Long Rifles.

Washington successfully maintained the siege, eventually forcing the British to  evacuate Boston by sea on March 17, 1776. Now, all of the 13 colonies were in  Patriot hands. The first phase of the Revolution was over, and the Americans had  triumphed completely . The next phase would begin in June 1776, when the British  forces, having been rested and re-equipped at Halif axe, Canada, invaded Long Island  to begin trying to conquer America.
