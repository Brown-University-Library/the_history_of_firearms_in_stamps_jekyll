Having evacuated Boston in March 1776, the British army sailed to Halifax, Canada. Reinforced, they planned to seize New York and its excellent harbor. General George Washington anticipated the move and brought the Continental Army there. On June 29, the British landed at Staten Island. A few days later in Philadelphia, the Continental Congress declared American independence. In August, the British landed in Brooklyn, on the western tip of Long Island, aiming to seize the East River crossings to Manhattan Island. In the last week of August, they defeated the Americans at the Battle of Brooklyn Heights, but George Washington managed to evacuate his army and supplies across the river to Manhattan.

Brooklyn would become a template for the rest of the war. In stand-up battles, the British could usually defeat the Americans. Yet as long as Washington could keep an army intact, the cause of Independence remained alive.
