This 1968 stamp also features a detail from Jonathan Trumbull’s Bunker Hill painting. Its use of color is far advanced from the monochrome stamps of the 1920s or 1950s, yet seems primitive compared to its 1975 counterpart.

The man in the foreground is an American officer, for he carries a sword but no long gun. Next to him is a rank and file militiaman, with simpler clothing and a musket. Black men fought on both sides of the American Revolution, and this stamp was the first to honor the contributions of Black Patriot fighters.
