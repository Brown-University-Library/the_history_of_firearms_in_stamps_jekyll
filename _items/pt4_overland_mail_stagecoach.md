The U.S. Post Office sometimes issues stamps about its own history, including the history of brave, armed postal delivery men.

The Overland Mail route, established in 1858, provided a land delivery route to California from the rest of the United States. It was much faster than oceanic mail shipping, which required going all the way south around Cape Horn, the southern tip of South America.

The map shows the Overland Mail route from central Missouri to San Francisco, traveling in part through the recently-acquired Gadsden Purchase.

The coach is being chased by hostiles of some sort. The driver attempts to steer the frightened and bucking horses. His companion, riding shotgun, tries to drive off the attackers.

Further reading: Wayne R. Austerman, _Sharps Rifles and Spanish Mules: The San Antonio-El Paso Mail, 1851-1881_ (College Station, Tex.: Texas A&M Univ. Pr., 1985).
