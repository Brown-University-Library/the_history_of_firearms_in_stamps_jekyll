The phrase “the shot heard round the world” comes from Ralph Waldo Emerson’s poem read at the dedication on the Concord Monument in 1826, as depicted on the preceding stamp. This baseball stamp presents the phrase in a different context.

In the 1951 baseball season, the New York Giants (today, San Francisco) had come from a 13 ½ game deficit to finish in a tie with the Brooklyn Dodgers (today, Los Angeles). A three-game playoff would determine the National League championship. In the final game, the Dodgers held a 4-2 lead, as the Giants batted in the bottom of the ninth inning. With two men on base, the Giants’ Bobby Thomson hit a three-run home run, winning the National League championship for the Giants. Thomson’s epic home run was instantly dubbed “the shot heard round the world,” and remains one of the most famous moments in baseball history.
