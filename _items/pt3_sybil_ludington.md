Another series in the Post Office’s bicentennial stamps was the four-stamp “Contributors to the Cause.” Sixteen-year-old Sybil Ludington was an ardent Patriot. Her father, Henry Ludington, led the local militia. When fifty pro-British Americans (“Loyalists”) came to the Ludington house to arrest him, Sybil lit candles and instructed her siblings to march around military-style. As in the movie “Home Alone,” the ruse scared off the attackers.

On the night of April 26, 1777, a messenger told the Ludingtons that the British army was burning Danbury, Connecticut. The local militia did not have enough men to stop them. So Sybil set out on a 40 mile night ride through the rain, to summon all the militia she could. On the way, some robbers attempted to have their way with her, but she drove them off with her father’s gun.

The hundreds of militiamen she raised couldn’t save Danbury, but they did defeat the British at the Battle of Ridgefield. For the rest of the war, the British never dared to venture far into Connecticut.
