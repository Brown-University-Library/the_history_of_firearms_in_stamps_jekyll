President Harry Truman issued Executive Order 9981, on July 26, 1948, abolishing racial segregation in the U.S. military. He ordered “equality of treatment and opportunity for all persons in the armed services without regard to race, color, religion or national origin.”

Truman had the power to do so because the U.S. Constitution makes the President commander-in-chief of the military, and so he can issue orders for its operations.

The stamp is part of the 2005 series “To Form a More Perfect Union,” honoring advances in civil rights in the decades after the World War II. In the view of more and more Americans, the hard-fought victories over the racist dictatorships in Germany and Japan showed the importance of ending racial discrimination by government within the United States. 

The soldiers’ rifles are not intended to be drawn realistically; the magazines are very far forward underneath the barrel.
