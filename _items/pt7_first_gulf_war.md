This stamp is part of the 1990s series in the “Celebrate the
Century” program. Like the Vietnam War, the First Gulf War, has
received only a little attention on U.S. stamps. The stamps for the
last five decades in the series were chosen by public ballot.

In August 1999, Iraqi dictator Saddam Hussein invaded and swiftly
conquered the small Persian Gulf nation of Kuwait. President
George H.W. Bush led the organization of a 39-nation coalition
against Saddam. On January 17, 1992, the coalition’s offensive
began, and swiftly drove Saddam out of Kuwait. Pursuant to the
terms agreed by the coalition, American forces did not seize the
opportunity proceed deep into Iraq and topple Saddam at his
moment of vulnerability. That decision would have profound
consequences in the next decade.
