To celebrate America 250, the Postal Service released the 15-stamp sheet Battlefields
of the American Revolution on April 16, 2026, at Minuteman National Historic Park, in
Concord, Massachusetts. Five battlefields are depicted, all of them involving battles that
were also subjects of stamps for America’s 150th or 200th anniversaries: Lexington &amp;
Concord (1775), Bunker Hill (1775), Trenton (1776), Saratoga (1777), and Yorktown
(1781). For each battlefield, there is one stamp with a photograph of the modern
memorial site, plus a watercolor diptych of a battle scene, by Greg Harlin.

The Thomas Paine quote, in the upper right, was published on Dec. 23, 1776, at the
nadir of the war. The “summer soldier” and the “sunshine patriot” had already given up.
The brave souls who, even “in this crisis” would stand with their country, would earn the
“thanks of man and woman.” Just three days after Paine wrote, with the American cause
on the brink of final defeat, George Washington and other brave souls crossed the
Delaware River and routed the British in a surprise attack at Trenton, thereby keeping
independence alive.
