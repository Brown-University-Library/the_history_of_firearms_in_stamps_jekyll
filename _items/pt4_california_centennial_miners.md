Very rapid mass migration to California began with the gold rush resulting from the 1849 discovery of gold  there. By the next year, California’s population was  large enough to qualify for statehood.

The migrants brought a tremendous variety of firearms. These guns were needed for the typical  purposes of frontier communities, such as hunting for  food and protection from Indians. They were also especially needed for self-defense from other  migrants, such as the criminal gangs of toughs from  Australia, who ran wild in San Francisco.

![An antique Russian 1838 muzzle-loading percussion musket with a dark wooden stock, long iron barrel, and a visible percussion lock mechanism](../img/itemimgs/russia_america_fur_co_1838_percussion_musket.jpg)

Traders from the Russian colony in Alaska ventured down the Pacific Coast, including to San Francisco,  where they sold firearms, among other goods. The  photo shows a [Russian America Fur Company model 1838 musket](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/a-prospering-new-republic-1780-to-1860/case-10-california-gold-rush/russia-america-fur-co-1838-percussion-musket.aspx), which has been converted from flintlock to percussion.
