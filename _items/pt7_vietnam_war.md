Other than the Civil War, no American was elicited so much controversy on the American home front as the War in
Vietnam--especially from 1968 onward, when it became clear that President Johnson’s optimistic claims about progress
had been deceitful.

This stamp is part of the 1960s series from the Postal Service’s 1998-99 “Celebrate the Century” megaseries.

Americans had air superiority throughout the war. Here, some soldiers have been helicoptered over the jungle and
delivered to a battle zone.

The American service rifle of the was the M16. It remains the service rifle today, modified into M4. Like the Like its
Korean War predecessor the M14, the M16 is an assault rifle, which means it is capable of selective fire. By using a
selector switch, the user can fire semi-automatically (one shot per trigger press), or automatically (multiple shots by
holding down the trigger).

The M16 was invented by the ArmaLite company in 1957. ArmaLite had been founded by aerospace engineers to use
the latest techniques to make firearm lighter. It was dubbed the AR-15, since it was ArmaLite’s 15th firearms model. In
1959, ArmaLite sold the patent to Colt’s manufacturing. It was capable of selective fire; that is, the user can fire the gun
either semi-automatically (one bullet per trigger press) or automatically (continuous fire as long as the trigger is held
down).

A semi-automatic only version was introduced to the American citizen market in 1965. Although the patents have
expired, Colt’s retains the right to the tradename for the “AR-15.” With various names, semi-automatic rifles based on the
AR platform have become the most popular firearm in American history, and now constitute about 20% of firearms olsde
in a typical year. They are loww recail, easy to handle, ergonomically excellent, and highly customizable. Howeer, their
bullets are so small that some states do not allow them for hunting deer or larger game.

In the early years, AR-15 and M16 were used interchangeably, although popular usage eventually settled on M16 for the
selective fire rifle, and [AR-15](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/wwii,-korea,-vietnam-and-beyond-1940-to-present/case-42-guns-of-vietnam-and-desert-storm/colt-ar-15-semi-automatic-rifle.aspx) for the semi-automatic only. The two firearms appear almost identical on the outside,
except for the selector switch on the M16. The M16 can fire at least four times as fast as the AR-15, due to differences in
internal components.
