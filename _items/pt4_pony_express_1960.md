The Post Office certainly was not going to ignore the 100th anniversary of the Pony Express in 1860.

On the one hand, the Pony Express was a great success. Letters for Western recipients were  collected at St. Joseph, Missouri, and then dispatched  station to station as far west as Sacramento,  California. The Pony Express chopped 10 days off the  time it took a letter from the East to reach California  via ship sailing around Cape Horn. The Pony Express  helped integrate the young State of California into the Union.

However, the Pony Express could never cover its costs, and it was discontinued upon the completion of  the transcontinental telegraph line in October 1861.
