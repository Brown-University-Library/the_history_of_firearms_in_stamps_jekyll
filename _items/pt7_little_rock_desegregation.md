Another stamp from the 2005 series “To Form a More Perfect Union.” In 1954 the U.S. Supreme Court unanimously ruled in _Brown v. Board of Education_ that assigning pupils to schools on the basis of their race violated the Fourteenth Amendment’s guarantee of “equal protection of law.” But many schools refused to comply.

When a U.S. district court ordered the end of segregation at Little Rock High School, in Arkansas’s capital city, Governor Orville Faubus defied the court order, and angry mobs backed him up.

To enforce the court order, President Dwight Eisenhower called the Arkansas National Guard into federal service and ordered it to protect the nine black students who had chosen to attend Little Rock High.

The National Guardsman’s rifle is not meant to be portrayed realistically, but Guard rifles of the time were usually Army surplus, such as the M14 selective fire.
