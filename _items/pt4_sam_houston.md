Having previously served as Governor of Tennessee, Sam  Houston led the Texan War of Independence to victory in 1836.  First in war and first in peace, he was elected first President of  the Republic of Texas. President Houston pursued a policy of  conciliation and friendship with the Comanche and other Indians,  who controlled most of central, western, and northern Texas.

When Texas joined the United States in 1845, Sam Houston  became one of the state's first two U.S. Senators. As Senator, he  opposed the Kansas-Nebraska Act in 1854, which opened up the  Kansas Territory to slavery. He presciently argued that the Act  would inflame sectional tensions, and he left the Democratic  Party over the issue.

Returning to the Texas governorship in 1859, he opposed  secession from the Union, and did his best, unsuccessfully, to  keep Texas from joining the Confederate States of America.
