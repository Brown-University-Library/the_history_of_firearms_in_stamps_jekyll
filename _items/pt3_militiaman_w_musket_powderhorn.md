This American militiamen looks much like the Continental Army soldier in the previous stamp, except that his uniform is less fancy—probably because he supplied his own clothes. The militiaman and the Continental Army soldier both carried the same types of guns, which they typically provided themselves.

The most common muskets for militia were the British Brown Bess or the French Charleville musket. The most visible difference is the Charleville’s three bands to secure the barrel to the stock. Lacking bands, the militiaman’s gun is a Brown Bess. The Charleville is discussed in the stamp titled “Trumbull: Burgoyne surrender.”
