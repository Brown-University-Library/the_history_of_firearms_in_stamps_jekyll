This stamp is based on a 1918 Marine recruiting poster by James
Montgomery Flagg. A prolific illustrator for popular magazines
such as Vanity Fair, Life, Collier’s, and the Saturday Evening Post,
Flagg produced several patriotic posters during World War I.

![U.S. military issue Colt Model 1911 semi-automatic pistol. The handgun features a dark blued finish on its slide and frame, a five-inch barrel, an exposed hammer, a smooth curved trigger, and signature checkered diamond-pattern walnut grip panels.](../img/itemimgs/u_s_colt_model_1911_semi_automatic_pistol_1_.jpg)

The Marine holds the standard service handgun of the period,
the recently-invented [Colt 1911 semiautomatic pistol](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/world-war-i-and-firearms-innovation/case-32-wwi-america-and-the-allies/us-colt-model-1911-semi-automatic-pistol-(1).aspx).
Chambered in .45 caliber, it had plenty of stopping power. As
fast as the user could press the trigger, it could fire 8 shots
before needing to be reloaded, and thanks to a detachable box
magazine in the grip, reloading took only a few seconds.

The Colt 1911 remained the standard U.S. military sidearm until
1984. Today, more than a century later, it remains one of the
most popular American handguns, with new models being made
based on the classic design. It was created by the greatest
firearms inventor of all time, Utah’s John Moses Browning.
