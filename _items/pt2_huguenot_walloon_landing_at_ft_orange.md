The French government persecuted French Protestants, the  
Huguenots. Many of them fled to Protestant nations, such as  
Great Britain or the United Provinces of the Netherlands.  
Although Huguenots would eventually settle all over the  
American colonies, the first immigrants came to the Dutch West  
India Company’s colony of New Netherland in 1624.

As of the 1620s, the Dutch republic was the firearms  
manufacturing center of the world, producing large quantities at  
affordable prices. The 1624 settlers presumably brought  
standard Dutch muskets. Later, the inhabitants of New  
Netherland would produce a first distinctively American firearm:  
the [Dutch-English fowler](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/road-to-american-liberty-1700-to-1780/case-4-shot-heard-round-the-world/dutch-english-flintlock-fowler.aspx), a hybrid musket that was suitable for  
militia service and for hunting, especially waterfowl. This was the  
beginning of the enduring American preference for multipurpose  
hybrid guns.
