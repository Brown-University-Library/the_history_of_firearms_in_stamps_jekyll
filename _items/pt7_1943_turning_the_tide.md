The 1943 pane includes another Willie & Joe
Illustration by Bill Mauldin. The lower right stamp
is “Marines assault Tarawa.” The November 1943
Amphibious invasion of the Tarawa Atoll, in the
Gilbert Islands, was to allow the creation of air
bases for the next phase of war, to take the
Marshall Islands, which in turn were needed for
offensives to the Philippines and the Mariana Islands.
In the island battles, close quarters combat with Japanese in fortified positions was
common.

![Winchester Model 1897 pump-action trench shotgun. The military firearm features a short 20-inch barrel almost entirely covered by a ventilated steel heat shield, an integrated bayonet adapter at the muzzle, an underlying tubular magazine, a grooved slide handle, and a dark walnut buttstock.](../img/itemimgs/u_s_winchester_model_1897_trench_shotgun.webp)

Shotguns were well-suited to the task; they are not suited for long-distance
firing, but are very powerful at short range. The picture is a pump action [Winchester
model 97 shotgun](https://www.nramuseum.org/guns/the-galleries/modern-firearms-1950-to-present/case-57-still-more-guns/winchester-model-1897-trench-shotgun.aspx), invented in 1897. It saw extensive use in the trenches of World War
I, and in World War II.
