Cartoonist Bill Mauldin covered World War II from the front lines.
His six cartoons per week were published in “Stars and Stripes,”
the U.S. military newspaper, and widely reprinted. The two
characters here, Willie and Joe, realistically and humorously
portrayed the grime and dirt of war, the enlisted man’s frustration
with officers, and other conditions showing that war was difficult
and not glamorous. Such honest cartoons were never permitted in
the newspaper of Axis dictatorships.

To today’s viewer, the most shocking aspect part of the picture
may be the cigarettes. Cigarettes were distributed freely to
soldiers during the war, as part of rations. Heavy smoking is bad
for health in the long run, but in the short run nicotine helps the
body resist fatigue and promotes calm and mental alertness.
