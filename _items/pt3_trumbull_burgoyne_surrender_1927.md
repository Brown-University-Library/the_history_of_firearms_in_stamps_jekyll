At the Battle of Saratoga, N.Y., on September 19 and  October 7, 1777, Burgoyne’s weakened British and  Hessian army suffered a crushing defeat. All of  Burgoyne’s army was taken prisoner. The scene  depicts Burgoyne handing over his sword, the  traditional commander’s gesture of surrender. When  news of Saratoga reached Paris, the French  government decided to abandon its policy of covertly  supplying arms to the Americans. Soon, the Franco American Treaty Amity and Commerce was  negotiated, and France entered the war as an active  American ally.
