The American Revolution began at Lexington  & Concord on April 19, 1775, when British  soldiers marched out from Boston to conduct  house-to-house searches for gunpowder and  firearms. The British won a quick victory at  Lexington, were repulsed at the Concord  Bridge, and nearly wiped out on their march  back to Boston, as armed Americans swarmed in front of the countryside.

This 1925 stamp is based on the mural The Birth of Liberty, by Canadian painter  Henry Sandman. It was the first stamp honoring the American Revolution, and  features fine engraving.
